# FAQ

### 1. How do the recommendations work?

Stack Audit reviews your team’s current AI tools, plan types, usage patterns, and overlap across vendors. It then recommends the most defensible cost actions first, such as downgrades, seat reductions, or removing duplicate subscriptions. Vendor switching is only suggested when the savings case is materially stronger.

### 2. Is AI making the pricing decisions?

No. Pricing logic and savings calculations are deterministic, rule-based, and tied to known plan assumptions. AI may help generate a readable summary, but it does not decide the numbers, savings estimates, or core procurement recommendations.

### 3. What happens to our data?

Stack Audit is designed to minimize exposure of sensitive information. Public share links are shaped for safe external sharing and exclude private lead details or internal identifiers. The goal is to let teams share savings insights without exposing unnecessary company data.

### 4. Why would you recommend switching vendors at all?

In many cases, we do not. The system is intentionally conservative and prefers keeping a working stack when the savings difference is small. Cross-vendor changes only appear when there is a clear overlap problem or a meaningful cost advantage that justifies the operational tradeoff.

### 5. Is this actually useful for early-stage startup teams?

Yes. Stack Audit is built for startups that adopted AI tools quickly without formal procurement review. It is especially useful for teams with 5 to 50 people, multiple paid AI subscriptions, and growing pressure to explain spend with finance-grade logic instead of rough guesses.
